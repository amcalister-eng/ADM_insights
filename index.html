Why this matters

From 10 December 2026, Tranche 1 of the Australian Privacy Act reforms introduce binding new obligations under APP 1.7–1.9. Woolworths must update its Privacy Policy to explicitly disclose every computer program that uses personal information to make — or substantially assist in making — a decision that could significantly affect an individual's rights or interests.

This covers positive and negative outcomes alike: offering a discount, ranking a candidate for interview, or suspending an Everyday Rewards account all trigger the same disclosure obligation.

⚖️

The APP 1 legal test — three criteria

1. The Tool
Technology involved?

Is a computer program making or substantially assisting a decision? Applies even when a human makes the final click.

2. The Data
Personal info used?

Personal information is interpreted broadly — any data that identifies an individual directly or indirectly, at any stage of the process.

3. The Impact
Rights affected?

Does the decision affect financial status, legal standing, employment, or access to services? Positive or negative — both count.

If all three criteria are met, disclosure in the Privacy Policy is mandatory.

🗓️

Two-phase delivery approach

Phase 1 · MVP Compliance

Rapid targeted execution focused on Tranche 1 PI-based ADM. Tactical audit → single source of truth → customer-centric Privacy Policy update. Target: 30 November 2026.

Phase 2 · Comprehensive Governance

Strategic expansion to include non-PI systems and employee-related tools. Prepares for proposed Tranche 2 reforms: Fair & Reasonable test, Right to Object, and amendments to the employee records exemption.

Key assumption: OAIC guidance expected early 2026 is consistent with current Tranche 1 understanding. PCT will monitor and update approach accordingly.

Key project assumptions
  • Executive mandate issued to all BUs confirming business responsibility for ADM compliance.
  • OAIC regulatory guidance (expected early 2026) is consistent with current Tranche 1 interpretation — PCT will monitor and update.
  • ADM scope is initially PI-only (Option 1). Estimated ~40% of Woolworths AI projects use personal information.
  • Employee records exemption is not relied upon — does not apply to prospective employees or contractors.
  • The ADM Register is extensible and compatible with future Group Technology AI Governance framework.
  • Ongoing compliance supported via existing forums: Privacy Artifacts Review Forum and AI roadmap SteerCos.
